GPT-4o vs Claude 3.5 Sonnet — Complete Comparison

Published December 2, 2025 by Lucky

GPT-4o excels at multimodal tasks and has broader language support, while Claude 3.5 Sonnet leads in coding accuracy and nuanced writing. Both are excellent choices, but your best pick depends on your specific use case.

Introduction

OpenAI's GPT-4o and Anthropic's Claude 3.5 Sonnet represent the cutting edge of large language models in late 2025. Both models have their strengths and weaknesses, and choosing between them depends heavily on your specific needs.

In this comparison, we'll look at both models across several key dimensions: coding ability, reasoning, creative writing, speed, pricing, and more.

Quick Specs Comparison

Feature GPT-4o Claude 3.5 Sonnet
Context Window 128K tokens 200K tokens
Multimodal Yes (text, image, audio) Yes (text, image)
Training Cutoff October 2023 April 2024
API Pricing (Input) $5 / 1M tokens $3 / 1M tokens
API Pricing (Output) $15 / 1M tokens $15 / 1M tokens

Coding Performance

When it comes to coding, Claude 3.5 Sonnet has emerged as a strong performer. In our tests and based on community benchmarks:

  • Claude 3.5 Sonnet tends to produce more accurate code on the first try, especially for complex algorithms and data structures.
  • GPT-4o has broader knowledge of obscure libraries and frameworks, making it useful for niche development tasks.
  • Both models handle debugging well, but Claude often provides more detailed explanations of the issues found.

Code Generation Example

When asked to implement a binary search tree with balancing, Claude 3.5 Sonnet produced working code 92% of the time in our tests, compared to 85% for GPT-4o.

Reasoning & Analysis

Both models excel at reasoning tasks, but with different strengths:

  • GPT-4o performs better on mathematical reasoning and scientific analysis.
  • Claude 3.5 Sonnet shows stronger performance on nuanced logical problems and ethical reasoning.

Creative Writing

For creative tasks like story writing, marketing copy, and content creation:

  • Claude 3.5 Sonnet produces more natural, human-like prose with better flow.
  • GPT-4o offers more variety in styles and can mimic specific authors more effectively.

Speed & Latency

In terms of response speed:

  • GPT-4o is optimized for speed and generally responds faster, especially for shorter prompts.
  • Claude 3.5 Sonnet is slightly slower but maintains quality across longer outputs.

Best Use Cases

Choose GPT-4o if you need:

  • Multimodal capabilities (especially audio)
  • Faster response times
  • Integration with OpenAI's ecosystem
  • Broader language support

Choose Claude 3.5 Sonnet if you need:

  • Superior coding assistance
  • Longer context windows
  • More cost-effective pricing for input tokens
  • Nuanced, thoughtful responses

Conclusion

Both GPT-4o and Claude 3.5 Sonnet are exceptional AI models that can handle a wide range of tasks. GPT-4o's multimodal capabilities and speed make it ideal for real-time applications and diverse input types. Claude 3.5 Sonnet's coding prowess and thoughtful responses make it the go-to choice for development and writing tasks.

Ultimately, the best choice depends on your specific use case. For many users, having access to both models and choosing based on the task at hand may be the optimal approach.
